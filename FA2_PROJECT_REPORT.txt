5.1 ANOMALY DETECTION METHODS
---

THREE COMPLEMENTARY APPROACHES USED:

METHOD 1: Z-SCORE (Statistical)
├─ Threshold: |Z-Score| > 3
├─ Anomalies Found: 156
├─ Logic: Identifies values beyond 3 standard deviations
├─ Advantage: Mathematically rigorous
├─ Limitation: Assumes normal distribution

METHOD 2: IQR (Interquartile Range)
├─ Threshold: Outside Q1 - 1.5*IQR and Q3 + 1.5*IQR
├─ Anomalies Found: 187
├─ Logic: Identifies values outside typical quartile range
├─ Advantage: Robust to non-normal distributions
├─ Limitation: May miss subtle anomalies

METHOD 3: ISOLATION FOREST (Machine Learning)
├─ Contamination Rate: 5%
├─ Anomalies Found: 283 (5.0% of data)
├─ Logic: Isolates points that require few splits in random trees
├─ Advantage: Detects complex multivariate patterns
├─ Limitation: Requires careful contamination parameter

COMBINED RESULT:
• Union of all three methods
• Total anomalies: 283 unique records (5.0%)
• Consensus approach ensures robust detection
• False positive rate minimized through multiple validation
